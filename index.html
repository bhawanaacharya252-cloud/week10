Welcome to Butwal

The Gateway to Lumbini — A City of Growth and Culture

About Butwal

Butwal is a dynamic city located in the Lumbini Province of Nepal. It serves as a major trade hub connecting the Terai plains with the hilly regions of Nepal. Known for its cleanliness, greenery, and rapid development, Butwal is one of the fastest-growing cities in the country.

Where is Butwal located?
Butwal lies in Rupandehi District, about 240 km west of Kathmandu and close to the Indian border town of Sunauli. It sits at the base of the Siwalik Hills and on the banks of the Tinau River.
Why is Butwal important?
Butwal is a key economic and transportation center in Nepal, linking highways to Pokhara, Kathmandu, and Lumbini. It’s known for its modern infrastructure and educational institutions.

Famous Places Near Butwal

Here are some of the most popular tourist attractions in and around Butwal:

Lumbini – Birthplace of Lord Buddha
Lumbini is just 30 km south of Butwal and is one of the most sacred sites in the world for Buddhists. It features the Maya Devi Temple, Ashokan Pillar, and beautiful monasteries built by different countries.
Manimukunda Park (Phulbari)
This beautiful park, believed to be the ancient palace of King Manimukunda Sen, is surrounded by greenery and historic ruins. It’s a perfect picnic and relaxation spot for families.
Siddhababa Temple
Located on the Butwal–Palpa highway, Siddhababa Temple is a revered Hindu shrine attracting pilgrims from all over Nepal. The temple sits on a hillside with scenic views of the Tinau River valley.

Travel & Stay in Butwal

Butwal is well connected by the East-West Highway and Siddhartha Highway. The nearest airport is Gautam Buddha International Airport, just a 35-minute drive away.

Visitors can find comfortable hotels, lodges, and guesthouses throughout the city, ranging from budget-friendly to luxury options.

History of Butwal

Historically, Butwal was a small trade settlement between India and Nepal’s hill regions. Its location along the Tinau River made it a natural market and resting point for travelers.

In recent decades, Butwal has transformed into a modern urban area, playing a crucial role in Nepal’s economy, education, and development.
